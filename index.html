Early Life

Michael Jackson was born in 1958 in Gary, Indiana. He showed an early interest in music and began performing at a young age, influenced by his family's musical background. Growing up in a strict household, his father, Joe Jackson, pushed him and his siblings to perform.

Jackson 5

Michael Jackson joined the Jackson 5 in 1964, alongside his brothers. As the lead singer, he gained fame for his impressive voice and stage presence. The group signed with Motown Records in 1968, releasing hits like:

  • "I Want You Back"
  • "ABC"
  • "I'll Be There"
  • "Dancing Machine"

Their success made them one of the most popular acts of the 1970s.
