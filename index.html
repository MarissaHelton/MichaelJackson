Michael Jackson rose to fame as a child prodigy in the Jackson 5, captivating audiences with his talent. Transitioning to a solo career, he became a global star with groundbreaking albums like Off the Wall and Thriller.

Early life

Michael Jackson was born in 1958 in Gary, Indiana. He showed an early interest in music and began performing at a young age, influenced by his family's musical background. Growing up in a strict household, his father, Joe Jackson, pushed him and his siblings to perform. By age 5, Michael was already singing and performing, and by 6, he became the lead singer of the Jackson 5, the group formed with his brothers.

Jackson 5

Michael Jackson joined the Jackson 5 in 1964, alongside his brothers. As the lead singer, he gained fame for his impressive voice and stage presence. The group signed with Motown Records in 1968, releasing hits like "I Want You Back" and "ABC." Their success made them one of the most popular acts of the 1970s. Michael's talent and charisma began to shine, setting the stage for his future solo career.
