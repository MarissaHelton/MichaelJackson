Jackson 5

Michael Jackson was a member of the Jackson 5, which gained worldwide success in the late 1960s and early 1970s. Their music was a blend of pop, soul, and Motown, and their hits include "I Want You Back," "ABC," and "I'll Be There."

Solo Career

Michael Jackson launched his solo career in 1971 and released iconic albums such as Off the Wall, Thriller, and Bad. His music transcended boundaries and influenced generations of artists worldwide.

  • Thriller (1982)

Timeline of Michael Jackson's Life

  • 1958: Michael Joseph Jackson is born on August 29th in Gary, Indiana.
  • 1964: Michael joins his brothers in the Jackson 5 at the age of six.
  • 1971: Michael begins his solo career while still performing with the Jackson 5.
  • 1979: Release of Off the Wall, marking a new direction in his career.
  • 1982: Release of the iconic album Thriller, which becomes the best-selling album of all time.
  • 1984: Michael wins 8 Grammy Awards in one night, a record for any artist.
  • 1987: Release of Bad, which spawns hits like "Smooth Criminal" and "Bad."
  • 1991: Release of Dangerous, a blend of pop, rock, and new jack swing.
  • 1993: Michael Jackson is honored with the "Artist of the Decade" award at the American Music Awards.
  • 2001: Release of Invincible, his last studio album.
  • 2009: Michael Jackson passes away on June 25th, leaving behind an enduring legacy.
  • 2016: The documentary Michael Jackson's Journey from Motown to Off the Wall is released.
